A Messy Start

When our team got together we all were very confused about who we are and what we had to do.

The team member with an ENTJ personality was very dominating.

Three of our team members with ISFP, INFP and ISFJ personalities were very shy and it was hard for them to communicate and talk openly.

The member with ESFJ personality was full of suggestions.

Later on personalities started to clash and get into arguments, as we all had different views and opinions. Communicating was difficult and having completely different perspectives towards the website didn't help either. Though the commander in the group was able to get through to some of the members like the defender and consultant, the others had completely different opinions. We didn't have any idea how to decide on anything.

Did it Work Out? Or was it a Complete Disaster?

It did actually; as unbelievable as this may sound, it worked out pretty well.

The commander in the group was commanding but also understood the value of others opinions. They took responsibility for time management and planning.

The adventurer's creativity turned out be a real asset.

The mediator kept an open mind and contributed immensely.

The consultant challenged the commander at times which worked as constructive criticism.

The defender kept the group together.

As everyone got to give their opinion freely and found a valuable place for themselves in the group, we got along quite well and the project was a success.

Everyone had their own voice and value in our group. In the end, we were like a strong fist that punched through all the obstacles that stood in our way.
